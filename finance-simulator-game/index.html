📚 Glossary (tap to learn the tricky words)

Simple explanations for words used in this simulator (made for a 10-year-old).

GDP (Gross Domestic Product)
The total value of everything a country makes and sells. If GDP is growing, the economy is getting bigger.
GDP Growth
How fast GDP is increasing (or decreasing). Positive is good growth; negative means the economy is shrinking.
Gini (Gini Coefficient)
A score for inequality. Higher means money is more unevenly shared; lower means it’s more equal.
Unemployment
The percentage of people who want jobs but can’t find one.
Treasury Balance
How much money the government has saved right now (like a bank account).
Tax Revenue
Money the government collects from taxes.
Net Revenue
Money left over after spending costs are paid. Positive means surplus; negative means deficit.
Capital Gains Tax
A tax on profit made when you sell something for more than you bought it for (like shares).
Business Tax
A tax paid by companies on the money they earn.
Wealth Tax
A tax based on how much wealth (big savings/assets) very rich people have.
Tax Enforcement
How strongly the government checks and catches people who try to avoid paying tax.
Tax Evasion
When people illegally hide money to pay less tax.
Capital Flight
When rich people or companies move money to other countries to avoid taxes or risk.
Public Services
Things the government pays for that everyone uses (like schools, hospitals, police).
Infrastructure
Big public projects that help the country work (roads, rail, power, internet).
Government Debt
Money the government owes because it borrowed in the past.
Debt Interest
Extra money paid because of debt—like interest on a loan.
Debt Reduction
Using money to pay back debt (reducing what the government owes).
Budget Surplus / Budget Deficit
Surplus = the government took in more than it spent. Deficit = it spent more than it took in.
Overall Approval
A simple average of how happy different groups are with your government.
Political Risk
How likely it is that your plans will face big trouble because people are unhappy or the economy is unstable.
Flight Risk
How likely it is that money will "fly away" to other countries (capital flight) if people feel taxes/risk are too high.
Migration Rate
How many people are moving into the country versus leaving it.
Laffer Curve
An idea that if tax is too low you collect little money, but if tax is too high people avoid it—so there can be a “middle” tax rate that collects the most.
